✈️***Airline Route Profitability Analysis**
Overview

This project analyzes U.S. domestic airline operations data to identify the most profitable round-trip routes for strategic airline investment. By combining flight operations, ticket pricing, and airport information, the analysis evaluates route performance based on profitability, passenger demand, operational efficiency, and investment recovery potential.

The objective is to provide data-driven recommendations for airline executives seeking to maximize return on investment while minimizing operational risks.

### Business Objective

An airline is planning to invest in new aircraft and expand its route network. The challenge is determining which routes offer the strongest balance of:

Revenue generation
Passenger demand
Flight frequency
Operational reliability
Long-term profitability

This analysis identifies the top-performing routes and estimates how quickly an airline can recover a $90 million aircraft investment.

Key Findings
High-demand routes consistently generated stronger revenue and profitability.
Route profitability depends on both fare pricing and passenger volume.
Airport congestion significantly impacts operational performance.
A balanced evaluation of profitability and efficiency provides better investment decisions than revenue alone.
Several routes demonstrated strong potential for rapid investment recovery.
Business Recommendations

Based on profitability, passenger demand, and operational efficiency, the analysis identified the top routes for future investment opportunities.

### Recommended actions:

Prioritize expansion on high-demand routes.
Monitor delay-prone airports to reduce operational costs.
Use profitability-based route scoring for future network planning.
Continuously track route-level KPIs to optimize performance.
